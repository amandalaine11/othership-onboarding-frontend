Required before you can work on the floor. Read the instructions for your city.

New York City

CPR certification is required before you can work on the floor — due by end of Week 8.

In the USA it MUST be through the Red Cross. The only approved courses are:

  • Basic Life Support
  • CPR/AED for Professional Rescuers

Must be in-person (a portion may be online). Renewed annually.

You will be reimbursed for approved courses only. Reimbursement is issued at the 3-month mark after hire, once onboarding is fully complete.

Honorary Agreements

Please read the full agreement before signing.

Honouring Othership's Hard Work. And Honouring You.

This agreement is intended to do two things:

  • Bring awareness to the time, energy and financial resources Othership has invested to create a world-class experience; and define what is unique about Othership
  • Create clarity around the Guide <> Othership relationship, our mutual responsibilities, and how the company plans to invest in — and honour — you

Some rules of the road

  • Once onboarding is complete you will gain access to a significant amount of proprietary — privately owned — material. This information will not be shared anywhere beyond your work at Othership, both during and after employment or engagement with the company.
  • You will not "represent" Othership outside the physical house without getting approval from your Lead Guide.
  • You understand what makes Othership unique and will not take these principles outside of the house unless getting approval.
  • We will honour you and give you the tools to grow within Othership.

The Othership Vision

Othership is changing the landscape of mental health by helping people learn to regulate their emotions in a community driven way that is fun and habit forming. We see a world where your journey to living a more meaningful life happens at Othership, driven through community support.

What Makes Othership Unique

  • Emotional Regulation Classes — first of its kind in the sauna and ice bath, tied to emotional themes such as Loving Kindness, Rose Thorn Bud, Forgiveness, Gratitude.
  • Combination of Elements — song choice, lighting changes, essential oils, script and movement — all working together to support the theme.
  • Social Experiences — building trusted community through decreased ego defences brought on naturally through the hot and cold.
  • Othership Breathwork Style — rollercoaster breath style where the cadence changes with the musical beat, following a DJ set.

What it Took to Create

Othership represents the cumulative efforts of dozens of people and thousands of hours of work — certified Sauna Masters and Breathwork Facilitators, hundreds of hours of retreats and therapy, thousands of guided ice baths and sauna experiences, and first-of-their-kind emotional regulation classes reviewed with 20+ therapists, hypnotists, and coaches.

By signing below, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this agreement and commit to honouring both Othership and yourself throughout your Guideship journey.

Crew Pass Booking Policy

Read carefully — these policies apply to your Crew Unlimited Pass and Friends & Family pass.

Welcome aboard our all access pass journey. We're excited to have you wielding the power of the Crew Unlimited Pass and the Friends + Fam pass as members of our crew. To ensure smooth sailing for all, here's your guide to booking and using these passes with #cellularcommitment top of mind.

Crew Unlimited Pass Policies

Grace Periods

When it comes to our crew, there's no wiggle room for grace cancellations or no-shows. Late cancels and no-shows will be noted and marked as penalties in our system.

Cancellations

If you need to cancel, make sure you do it within the 12-hour window. This gives everyone a chance to access our programming and services.

Booking Window — Standby Policy

All Crew members adhere to the Standby Policy: paying guests take priority. If there is one spot left, staff can be let in if that spot is still available when class begins. If there are 5 or more spots left, staff may be added upon arrival. Staff cannot be placed on the MT Standby List, nor can holds be placed on behalf of crew.

Special Events

For special events, please confirm with Shannon Bent the type of credit required. Your comp Crew Pass should not be used to book into events unless confirmed otherwise.

Late Cancel Fee

A $35 fee applies if you cancel after the grace period.

No Show Fee

A $35 fee will be charged for no-shows. Let's keep the ship tight by respecting our shared commitments.

Friends + Fam Pass

The same policies apply. A loss of credit is applied in the case of a no-show or late cancellation.

Your understanding and commitment to these guidelines help us keep the ship sailing smoothly. #cellularcommitment

Privacy & Confidentiality

Required reading before going floor-ready.

At Othership, we hold space for deeply personal experiences. As a Guide, you are trusted with sensitive information and intimate moments. This trust is sacred.

Your Responsibilities

  • Everything shared in the space — by Journeyers or fellow crew — stays in the space.
  • You may not share, post, or discuss guest experiences, names, or personal details outside of Othership without explicit consent.
  • Proprietary training materials, class scripts, and operational processes are confidential and may not be shared outside your work at Othership.
  • Photography and video of guests requires explicit consent.
  • Social media posts referencing Othership, its sessions, or guests must be approved by your Lead Guide before posting.

Breaches of Confidentiality

Any breach of these guidelines is taken seriously and may result in immediate offboarding. If you are unsure whether something is appropriate to share, check with your Lead Guide first.

Code of Conduct

Othership's standards for every guide, every shift.

Being a Guide at Othership is a privilege. These standards exist to protect the experience — for Journeyers, for your crew, and for yourself.

Professionalism

  • Arrive on time, changed, and ready to punch in at your scheduled start.
  • Communicate proactively — if you need to drop a shift, give as much notice as possible and find your own coverage.
  • Your phone stays out of the session space during shifts.

Guest Experience

  • Every Journeyer deserves a world-class experience, every single time.
  • Issues with guests are escalated to the Ship Captain — do not handle them alone.

Crew Culture

  • Treat every crew member with respect, care and professionalism.
  • Celebrate each other. This crew is your community.

Safety

  • Your CPR/AED certification must be valid at all times while working on the floor.
  • Know the emergency protocols for your location before your first shift.
  • Report any safety concerns to the Ship Captain or Lead Guide immediately.
